Bike Resources

Rutgers Triathlon Riding!



Generally, our bike rides are outside during the warmer months, but during the winter we also do trainer rides at the Livingston Recreation Center.

If you're new to biking, the following is a set of guidelines and required and optional items for a bike ride.

Do before every ride!

ABC Bike Checklist -

  • Air - Check the air pressure in your tires and ensure they are inflated to the correct pressure

  • Brakes - Ensure your brakes are in working order and not worn out. Test the brakes that they actually slow you down

  • Chain - Examine your chain for any damage or debris and confirm it is clean and lubricated


Check the weather! Ride when it's dry and avoid severe windy conditions

Required items

  • Bike - Road bikes are the norm with some TT bikes on the team. Beginners can opt for a mountain bike or a hybrid too!

  • Helmet - Protect that Rutgers educated brain!

  • Cycling Kit - Suited for the weather with nifty back pockets to hold snacks.

  • Water bottle(s) - Helps you stay hydrated during the ride! Make sure your bike has bottle racks.

  • Snacks - If you're planning to ride for more than an hour, bring some snacks!

  • Cellphone - In the event you get lost, emergency, or bike flats/maintenance issues, give a good buddy a call.

  • Spare Tubes - If you get a flat ~25 mi from home, you'll need to change it. Flats happen, bring two of these.

  • Pump or CO2 with adapter - Once you change the tube, you'll need to inflate it with these great tools

  • Tire Levers - Necessary tools for removing the tire while changing a flat. Two is recommended

  • Multi-tool - Convenient arrangement of allen keys, wrenches and a chain tool. If something feels loose during your ride, can be used to make an adjustment

  • Cash/Credit Card/Wallet - Great for the inevitable moments when you run out of snacks or hydration.

  • Identification - Driver's license, RoadID, etc are always a good idea to keep on you in the event of a serious accident. Stay safe!

  • Keys - Don't get locked out from your car or house!


Optional Items

  • Saddle Bag - Highly recommeded, but technically optional. Can be attached to the back of your seat or on your top tube. Great place to keep spare tubes, CO2 cartridges, snacks, $20 for emergencies, etc.

  • Cycling clipless (clip in) pedals - Improves the power transfer efficiency from your legs to the bike. Makes you go faster! (Everyone falls once)

  • Cycling cleats & shoes - These are used to clip your legs into the pedals. Cleats will eventually wear out.

  • Light Jacket - Useful in case it rains or gets cold

  • Cycling Gloves - Acts as pads for your hands on the handlebars and can help protect your hands in the event of a crash

  • Sunglasses - Protects your eyes from wind and dust and other debris and keep you looking fresh.

  • Cycling Computer & Sensors - Attached to your bike. Appropriate sensors can be connected to measure cadence, speed and distance.

  • Watch - Useful for keeping track of splits, distance, etc. (If it's not on Strava, did you actually bike?)

  • Bike Lock - Can be useful if you stop somewhere and you can't bring your bike in / no one to watch the bikes.

  • Lights - Blinking lights are always a great idea if you're biking at night. Alternatively, don't bike at night; stay safe!

Local bike routes

While loops of Johnson Park are always possible, we do recommend venturing out and exploring the area on your bike!

  • Busch and Livi Campus loops
    11.3 mi / 311 ft / Easy - Used for beginner no drop rides; easy and flat with minimal traffic and traffic lights. Starts on Busch campus in front of Werblin Recreation Center (alternatively can start in Johnson Park) and goes through the Busch sideroads and goes into Johnson Park (speed bumps), swinging around to the far side of Livi and looping through then back to Werblin.

  • Flat Canal
    30.3 mi / 525 ft / Easy - Used for beginner long rides; easy and flat with some traffic and numerous traffic lights, recommended to start early in the morning. Multiple turn around points to shorten the ride. Starts on Busch campus in front of Werblin Recreation Center and goes through River Rd towards Bround Brook. Cut across the river towards Canal Rd and take it to Colonial Dr to Canal Rd then Hamilton St back to the river and into Johnson Park. Finish with a Johnson loop then back to Werblin

  • A Joyful Variant (1 hill)
    18.1 mi / 752 ft / Medium - Intro to Joyful; mostly flat with one joyful hill. Some traffic and numerous traffic lights, recommended to start early in the morning. Starts on Busch campus in front of Werblin Recreation Center and goes through River Rd towards Bround Brook. Turn on to Middlebrook Rd then right to Washington Ave and climb. Take Hillcrest Rd then downhill on Mountain Ave back to River Rd and back to Werblin

  • A Joyful Variant (2 hill)
    26.0 mi / 1233 ft / Medium - Main hill plus one; flat warmup then 2 hills. Some traffic, not much shoulder and numerous traffic lights, recommended to start early in the morning. Starts on Busch campus in front of Werblin Recreation Center and goes through River Rd towards Bround Brook. Turn on to Middlebrook Rd then right to Washington Ave and climb. Continues to King George Rd then climb again! Swing around Mt Horeb Rd to Washington Valley Rd. Continue downhill through Chimney Rock park. Left onto Thompson Rd takes you over the highway and back to River Rd and back to Werblin.

  • A Joyful Classic
    29.6 mi / 1621 ft / Medium - Main hill plus rest; flat warmup then rolling hills for 15 mi. Some traffic, not much shoulder and numerous traffic lights, recommended to start early in the morning. Starts on Busch campus in front of Werblin Recreation Center and goes through River Rd towards Bround Brook. Turn on to Middlebrook Rd then right to Washington Ave and climb. Continues to King George Rd then climb again! Swing around Ferguson Rd then up Mt Horeb Rd King George Rd. Continue downhill then one more climb then downhill towards River Rd and back to Werblin. Left onto Thompson Rd takes you over the highway and back to River Rd and back to Werblin
